01 / the question

Is the evidence consistent with the claim?

A phone alone doesn't prove an accident. Merklit proves individual facts about the claimant, vehicle, place and time, then combines independent facts to support, contradict or qualify the incident narrative. Weak evidence routes to human review, never automatic rejection.

[01]

Reduce ambiguity

Separate verified observations from claimant statements and unavailable evidence.

[02]

Strengthen provenance

Bind fresh media, time, location, actor and device evidence into one tamper-evident record.

[03]

Support review

Route contradictions and weak evidence to human review instead of automatic claim decisions.

06 / integrity and legal boundary

Tamper evidence, correctly framed.

The integrity layer proves what was committed, when it existed and whether it changed. It does not turn a device, sensor or blockchain into a truth oracle.

The proof establishes

The exact evidence commitment existed by a recorded block.

A later change would produce a different hash.

The public anchor can be checked independently of Merklit.

The proof never establishes

That GPS or another sensor input was truthful.

That the claimant personally held the device.

That an accident occurred merely because a hash exists.

[ Legal boundary ]

The export is a court-supporting electronic evidence package, not guaranteed admissibility. Admissibility and evidentiary weight remain subject to proper procedure, case facts and legal review.

Encryption

AES-256-GCM per evidence object, tenant-specific KMS keys and optional BYOK.

On-chain policy

PII, raw media, policy numbers and coordinates never enter the blockchain.

Disclosure

Regional results by default. Exact location and full reports require an audited escalation.

Legal package

Manifest, signatures, licensed-CA time, inclusion proof, audit trail and a configurable Section 63 workflow.
